The M198 Howitzer

Problem

Required math: ordinary differential equations.

Required physics: Newton's laws of motion.

An M198 howitzer situated on a flat surface fires an M107 shell of mass $m$ at an angle $\theta$ to the horizon. The projectile leaves the howitzer's barrel at the coordinates $(x_0, y_0)$ with initial velocity $v_0$. During it's flight it is subjected to a drag force acting in the opposite direction to its motion $$\mathbf{F}_d = -\frac{1}{2}C\rho Av^2\hat{\mathbf{v}} $$ where $\rho$ is the density of the air, $C$ is the projectile's drag coefficient, $A$ is the projectile's area subject to the drag force, and $v$ is the projectile's velocity. $\hat{\mathbf{v}}$ is a unit vector in the direction of the projectile's velocity. Values for the constants are given below in the input section.

What is the $x$-coordinate of the projectile's landing spot?

Input

Constant Value Comments
$g$ $9.80665 \; \mathrm{m/s^2}$ Standard gravity
$\rho$ $1.225 \; \mathrm{kg/m^3}$ Density of air at sea level and 15Â°C
$m$ $43 \; \mathrm{kg}$ M107 155mm shell mass
$A$ $188.7 \; \mathrm{m^2}$ Exposed area of M107 155mm shell. Calculated using $A=\pi r^2$ where $r$ is the radius of a shell ($155/2 \; \mathrm{mm}$).
$C$ $0.0579$ Drag coefficient of M107 155m shell for speeds below mach 0.9.

The firing angle $\theta$ in degres, initial muzzle velocity $v_0$ in m/s, and coordinates of the howitzer's bore $(x_0, y_0)$ in meters. All to four significant figures.

Example input
# theta v_0 x_0 y_0

Output

The $x$-coordinate of the projectile's landing spot in meters, accurate to at least the nearest meter.
