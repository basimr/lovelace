Notes

  • This problem is inspired by the second example in the famous book The C Programming Language.
  • The Celsius and Farenheit scales read the same temperature at -40! This is one of the test cases.
  • Neither Celsius or Fahrenheit are good for science as a temperature of 0 degrees still means that some thermal energy exists. Scientists use the Kelvin scale which is 0 degrees Kelvin (K) at absolute zero (-273.15°C), the temperature at which all molecular motion ceases. Interestingly, the third law of thermodynamics says it's impossible to reach absolute zero.
  • Weird stuff: You can have weird physical systems with negative temperatures. Some systems still have some energy at absolute zero due to zero-point energy in quantum mechanics.
