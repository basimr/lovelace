As you learned in DNA transcription, the first step in converting DNA to proteins is transcribing the DNA into RNA. The second step is translating the RNA into a chain of amino acids, called a polypeptide. The process is biochemically complex but the resulting amino acid sequence is quite simple: each set of three base pairs (called a nucleotide triplet or codon) is translated to a single amino acid.
