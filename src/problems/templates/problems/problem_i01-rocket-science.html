We'll get you familiar with how Project Lovelace problems work by doing some rocket science! A rocket, say the Saturn V, needs to escape a planet with velocity $v$ but it needs to know how much fuel to carry with it for its escape. You can calculate the amount of fuel needed with the rocket equation $m_\mathrm{fuel} = M \left( e^{v/v_e} - 1\right)$ where $M = 250,000 \mathrm{kg}$ is the mass of the rocket (with no fuel), $v_e = 2,550 \mathrm{m/s}$ is the exhaust velocity of the rocket, and $e = 2.71828...$ is Euler's number. Your program or script should read in the velocity $v$ as an input and print the mass of fuel needed.
