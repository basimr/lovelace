The number π=3.1415926535897... is the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter and shows up absolutely everywhere in math and science. It's decimal expansion goes on forever but we can actually calculate it by summing up a bunch of fractions $$ \frac{\pi}{4} = 1 - \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{5} - \frac{1}{7} + \frac{1}{9} - \frac{1}{11} + \dots = \sum_{k=0}^n \frac{(-1)^k}{2k+1} $$ By adding more and more terms, you get a better and better approximation to π.
