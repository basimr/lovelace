Ever since the invention of writing ancient civilizations have sent written messages amongst themselves, and it was commonly the case that they wanted these messages to remain secret lest they fall into enemy hands. A famous method of encrypting a message is the Caesar cipher, named after the Roman general Julius Caesar who used it to secure military communications. It involves using a shifted alphabet instead of the regular alphabet. It is a very weak cipher that provides no security nowadays. As you will show, knowledge of a single word is enough to break the cipher and reveal the secret message.
