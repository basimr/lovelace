We'll try to get you familiar with how Project Lovelace problems work by doing some rocket science! A rocket, say the Saturn V, needs to leave a planet with velocity $v$ but it needs to know how much fuel to carry with it. Luckily you can calculate this with the rocket equation $m_\mathrm{fuel} = M \left( e^{v/v_e} - 1\right)$ where $M = 250,000 \mathrm{kg}$ is the mass of the empty rocket and $v_e = 2,550 m/s$ is the exhaust velocity of the rocket. Your program should read in the velocity $v$ as an input and print the mass of fuel needed.
